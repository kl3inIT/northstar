# IELTS Writing rubric (condensed public band descriptors)

Criteria (equal weight, graded independently):

- **TR/TA** — Task Response (Task 2) or Task Achievement (Task 1). Use the key
  `TR` for opinion/discussion/problem essays, `TA` for report/letter tasks.
- **CC** — Coherence and Cohesion.
- **LR** — Lexical Resource.
- **GRA** — Grammatical Range and Accuracy.

## Band anchors per criterion

### TR / TA

- **8**: fully addresses all parts; a clear, well-developed position;
  ideas extended and supported with relevant, specific examples.
- **7**: addresses all parts; clear position throughout; main ideas extended
  and supported, but development may be uneven or examples slightly general.
- **6**: addresses all parts though some more fully than others; position is
  relevant but conclusions may be unclear or repetitive; some ideas
  inadequately developed or examples missing.
- **5**: addresses the task only partially; position unclear or mechanical;
  ideas limited, underdeveloped, or with irrelevant detail; may drift
  off-topic or noticeably under length (Task 2 < 250 words, Task 1 < 150).

### CC

- **8**: information sequenced logically; all cohesion used naturally;
  paragraphing sufficient and appropriate.
- **7**: logical organisation and clear progression; a range of linking used
  flexibly with occasional over/under-use; one clear central idea per
  paragraph.
- **6**: coherent arrangement with overall progression; linking is effective
  but mechanical or faulty at times ("Firstly... Secondly..." scaffolding);
  referencing sometimes unclear; paragraphing present but not always logical.
- **5**: some organisation but lacking overall progression; linking
  inadequate, inaccurate, or over-used; repetitive because of weak
  referencing; paragraphing may be inadequate or missing.

### LR

- **8**: wide vocabulary conveying precise meanings; skilful use of uncommon
  items with only rare slips in word choice or collocation.
- **7**: sufficient range for some flexibility and precision; uses less
  common items with some awareness of style and collocation; occasional
  errors in word choice or word formation.
- **6**: adequate range for the task; attempts less common vocabulary but
  with some inaccuracy; errors in spelling or word formation that do not
  impede communication.
- **5**: limited but minimally adequate range; noticeable errors in spelling
  or word formation that may cause some difficulty; frequent repetition of
  the same words.

### GRA

- **8**: wide range of structures; most sentences error-free; only very
  occasional errors or inappropriacies.
- **7**: a variety of complex structures; frequent error-free sentences;
  good control with a few errors persisting.
- **6**: a mix of simple and complex forms; errors in complex structures but
  they rarely reduce clarity; some punctuation faults.
- **5**: limited range of structures; complex sentences attempted but they
  tend to break down; frequent grammatical and punctuation errors that can
  cause some difficulty for the reader.

Interpolate half bands (e.g. 6.5) when an essay sits between two anchors on
a criterion. An essay can score very differently across criteria — do not
let one criterion pull the others (halo effect).

## Calibration exemplars

Excerpt graded **TR 5, GRA 5** (position unclear, structures break down):

> Nowadays, many people is thinking that the technology have many advantage
> and disadvantage. In one hand it help us to communicate with the other
> people who is living far. In other hand some people become addict. So I
> think technology is good and also bad for the society.

Why: no clear position ("good and also bad"), ideas listed but not
developed (TR 5); subject-verb agreement errors throughout, article errors,
formulaic linking used inaccurately ("In one hand") (GRA 5, CC 5).

Excerpt graded **TR 7, GRA 7** (clear position, complex forms controlled):

> While remote work undeniably offers flexibility, I would argue that its
> greatest cost is invisible: the gradual erosion of informal mentorship.
> Junior employees, who once absorbed professional norms simply by
> overhearing negotiations or watching a manager defuse a conflict, now
> learn in isolation. A survey my company ran last year illustrates this —
> new hires who onboarded remotely asked 40% fewer questions.

Why: a clear, arguable position extended with a specific (personal-scale)
example (TR 7); varied complex structures — concessive opening, embedded
relative clauses — essentially error-free (GRA 7-8); "erosion of informal
mentorship", "defuse a conflict" show precise, less common lexis (LR 7-8).
